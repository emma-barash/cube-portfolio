Emma Ayamé Barash

Ayamé (アヤメ) means "iris" in Japanese.

I was born in a small town called Ukiah, two hours north of San Francisco, California. All my life, I have ridden horses, backpacked for weeks at a time in the high Sierras, and developed a deep interest in motorcycles and performance cars. After my senior year, I moved to Newport Beach, California, to attend a night class in car design and to work at my cousin's successful startup company. In January of 2018, I began my first semester at Brandeis University, where I quickly became involved in academics, as well as the robotics club and the equestrian club. In college, I had my first formal exposure to computer science for which I took a profound interest. My second year, I enrolled in an introductory course for programming in Java and though I enjoyed the material, I felt lost in the class, which consisted of a sea of over one hundred individuals.

In order to pursue practical usage of coding, I have taken a sabbatical from Brandeis to attend an intensive web development program. This past May, I graduated and received a certificate in full stack web development from V School, located in Salt Lake City.
